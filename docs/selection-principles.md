# Portfolio Selection Principles

This portfolio was intentionally curated around real materials and chemistry problems rather than generic demonstration notebooks.

## What Was Included

- projects that show actual simulation ownership
- workflows that move from setup to interpretable result
- evidence of method breadth across molecular, periodic, and MD simulation
- code and infrastructure work that support reproducible computational research

## What Was Excluded

- raw folders that only make sense to the original researcher
- incomplete output dumps without interpretation
- overly broad lists of tools without case-study context
- toy examples that would weaken the credibility of the portfolio

## Why This Matters

For computational chemistry hiring, a portfolio should demonstrate:

- the scientific question
- the method choice
- the workflow logic
- the interpretive outcome
- the ability to communicate technical work clearly
